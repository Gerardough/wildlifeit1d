Forest Conservation

Public funding for forests is significantly lower than subsidies that harm the environment. For instance, at least 100 times more public funding goes to environmentally harmful subsidies than to finance for forests. Additionally, Indigenous Peoples and local communities, who are crucial in protecting tropical forests, receive only a fraction of the finance they need to effectively manage their territories.

Earth Hour

During Earth Hour 2024, France achieved a significant reduction in electricity usage, saving a total of 132.11 megawatts (MW), which is 110 percent higher than the previous year’s savings. This demonstrates the collective impact of individuals and communities participating in the event.

Mekong Fish

One-fifth of the Mekong Fish species are now threatened with extinction. The Mekong’s fish populations are vital for the health, food security, and livelihoods of tens of millions of people across the region, as well as for the overall health of the river system. Despite their importance, these fishes continue to be undervalued and overlooked by decision-makers, with at least 19% of assessed species estimated to be heading towards extinction, and 18 species already listed as Critically Endangered

Biodiversity

WWF’s 2020 Living Planet Report presents a grim picture, revealing an average 68% drop in mammal, bird, fish, reptile, and amphibian populations since 1970. This decline is largely attributed to habitat destruction driven by unsustainable agriculture and logging practices.

Whale Safety

Collaborative efforts are being made to ensure whale migrations are safe. This is crucial for ocean health, and tools are being developed to help protect these magnificent creatures during their migrations
