The Members

Board Members

Carter Roberts

President and CEO

Roberts leads WWF’s initiatives to preserve the planet’s major ecosystems and combat climate change by integrating science, field, and policy programs with strategic partnerships with markets and businesses to reduce their environmental impact.

Loren Mayor

Chief Operating Officer

At WWF, Loren oversees operational functions, resource mobilization teams, and strategic partnerships. Prior to WWF, Loren was President of Operations at National Public Radio (NPR) and held leadership roles at PBS and The Corporation for Public Broadcasting.

Nik Sekhran

Chief Conservation Officer

As the Chief Conservation Officer at WWF, Nik Sekhran brings over 26 years of experience in environmental protection and sustainable development. He has worked in over 45 countries and previously served as the Director for Sustainable Development at the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP).

Jocelyn Starzak

Senior Vice President Oceans

Serving as Senior Vice President and General Counsel, Jocelyn Starzak provides legal guidance across WWF’s operations. With over 25 years of experience, she advises on partnerships, governance, and compliance, and has previously established the legal department at the World Resources Institute.

Johan Bergenas

Senior Vice President Private Sector Engagement

Johan Bergenas is the Senior Vice President of Oceans at WWF-US, leading global programming for ocean health, climate resilience, and environmental security across various regions and sectors. His background includes work at Vulcan and co-founding a technology start-up.

Sheila Bonini

Senior Vice President Forests

Sheila Bonini, the Senior Vice President of Private Sector Engagement, oversees WWF’s corporate partnerships aimed at conservation. She has a history of driving sustainability in supply chains and previously led The Sustainability Consortium as CEO.
Goals

The World Wildlife Fund (WWF) is dedicated to advocating for the conservation of nature and reducing the most pressing threats to the diversity of life on Earth. The mission is to build a future where humans live in harmony with nature, aiming to save a planet rich in life, both in spirit and in living wonder of nature.
